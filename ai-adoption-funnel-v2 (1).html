01
MARKET · GOVERNANCE · SPEED

Incentive Compulsion Collides with Governance Capacity

STRUCTURAL ISSUE 1 — BROADEST
ADAPTABILITY FLEXIBILITY

Market adoption outpaces governance. Firms gain speed advantage while regulatory frameworks, judicial doctrine, and institutional credentialing lag. A rational system at the firm level becomes misaligned at the societal level — and that misalignment is an ethical problem, not just an efficiency one. When governance failure becomes politically visible, legislatures respond with blunt, poorly calibrated statutes that work for existing tools, not future ones.

02
LIABILITY · GENERATIONS · DOCTRINE

Shared Doctrinal Uncertainty: Who Bears the Risk

STRUCTURAL ISSUE 2
FLEXIBILITY RESILIENCE

Liability doesn't resolve through contractual reallocation. The difference across parties is capital endurance — who can survive the litigation queue longest absorbs the cost of doctrinal resolution. Exposure follows generational position: Gen Z enters at the most legally exposed position, Millennials administer compliance systems they didn't design, and Gen X structures deployment frameworks that determine how liability flows downward.

03
CAPITAL · METRICS · LEGITIMACY

Capital Scarcity, Measurement Blindness & Legitimacy Erosion

STRUCTURAL ISSUE 3
RESILIENCE IMAGINATION FLEXIBILITY ADAPTABILITY

Capital scarcity and measurement failure produce the same consequence: institutions unable to govern what they have permitted. Standard labor metrics cannot detect bifurcation — a society can simultaneously produce data showing employment health and a lived experience of precarity, and both can be true. Governing through frameworks that cannot detect the split actively delays the institutional response the data cannot yet surface.

04
FORMATION · PIPELINE · INDIVIDUAL

Career Ladder Compression & Professional Formation

STRUCTURAL ISSUE 4 — NARROWEST
RESILIENCE IMAGINATION

Routine cognitive tasks compress first, eliminating the apprenticeship substrate through which junior professionals develop judgment. The formation sequence — learn, practice, earn trust, gain authority — breaks down at its earliest steps. AI substitutes adequately today, masking the deficit until firms need senior professionals capable of auditing and governing AI systems at scale, only to find the pipeline was eliminated a decade earlier.
